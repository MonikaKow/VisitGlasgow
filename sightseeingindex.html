Top Attractions in Glasgow

Hover over the image to learn more!

Kelvingrove Art Gallery and Museum is the most popular free-to-enter visitor attraction in Scotland, which houses one of Europe’s great art collections.
Glasgow Botanic Gardens is located in the heart of the city’s West End by the River Kelvin and contains a variety of plant collections and riverside walks as well as the famous Kibble Palace.
The University of Glasgow has been a home for world-changing research and teaching for more than 560 years.
The first stone built Glasgow Cathedral was dedicated in the presence of King David I in 1136. The worship of God has been carried out within its walls for more than 800 years.
